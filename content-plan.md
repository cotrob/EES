### Page: `/laboratory/facility`  — Research Facility (TestCell)

**Page Title:** Research Facility
**Subtitle:** Purpose-built for real-world thermal comfort and energy testing

#### Hero / Intro

We built a full-scale test cell to develop and validate our products under real conditions — not just in theory.

Our TestCell is a real room (3.6m × 4.8m × 3.0m high) constructed to Australian Standard specifications with R2.0 batt insulation. It features a 7.5m² clear double-glazed, north-facing glass unit to commercial grade — designed to simulate worst-case solar gain.

#### What We Measure

The TestCell is equipped with professional-grade instrumentation aligned to international comfort and performance standards:

- **ASHRAE-55 / ISO 7730 Comfort Carts** — measuring operative temperature and thermal comfort
- **Heat Flux Meters** — quantifying radiant and conductive energy transfer through surfaces
- **Air Velocity Metering** — tracking airflow patterns within the space
- **Air Temperature Stratification Strings** — mapping temperature differences from floor to ceiling
- **Ventilation Air Change Rate Monitoring** — verifying fresh air delivery and infiltration
- **Thermal Imaging Cameras** — visualising surface temperatures and identifying heat loss

#### What We Do

We use the TestCell for:

- Full thermal comfort evaluation of our products (heating and cooling)
- Energy efficiency benchmarking under controlled and real-world conditions
- Development and tuning of control systems
- Independent third-party testing for external clients

**Interested in third-party testing?** Contact us for more information.

### Page: `/laboratory/simulation-room` — Simulation Room

**Page Title:** Simulation Room
**Subtitle:** Controlled testing under real-world conditions

> *Note: The TestCell IS the simulation room. This page can either redirect to `/laboratory/facility` or expand with more detail on the testing methodology. Suggested content below expands on the methodology angle.*

#### Real Conditions, Measured Results

Our simulation room replicates the thermal behaviour of a real occupied space — not a sealed box in a lab. With a north-facing 7.5m² glass wall, insulated walls to Australian standard, and full environmental monitoring, we test how products actually perform in conditions people live and work in.

#### Testing Standards

All evaluations are conducted in accordance with:

- **ASHRAE Standard 55** — Thermal Environmental Conditions for Human Occupancy
- **ISO 7730** — Ergonomics of the thermal environment

#### How Testing Works

1. **Baseline measurement** — we characterise the room's thermal behaviour without any conditioning system active
2. **System installation** — the product under test is installed as it would be in a real building
3. **Controlled scenarios** — we run heating, cooling, and mixed-mode tests across a range of outdoor conditions
4. **Data collection** — all sensors log continuously, capturing comfort metrics, energy consumption, and system response times
5. **Analysis & reporting** — results are compiled into a performance report with recommendations

### Page: `/laboratory/hydronic-ceiling-panels` — Hydronic Ceiling Panels

**Page Title:** Hydronic Ceiling Panels
**Subtitle:** Heating, cooling and a finished ceiling — in one system

#### Overview

Our suspended hydronic ceiling tiles are modular and versatile. They are capable of both **heating and cooling** and are designed to accept a range of finishing surfaces. They are easy to install and simple to operate.

Each tile delivers three functions in one:

1. **An insulative barrier** — directly opposite to where the conditioning occurs
2. **A highly responsive conditioning system** — embedded within the tile itself
3. **A finished architectural surface** — providing the desired ceiling appearance

This three-in-one design is what makes the system cost-effective.

#### Why Radiant Ceiling Systems?

The human body exchanges energy with its environment through three mechanisms: **radiation**, **convection**, and **exhalation** (breathing). All three work together to establish thermal comfort.

**Radiant transfer has the greatest impact on comfort.** Radiant systems are the most effective form of conditioning because they deliver both radiant and convective energy transfer simultaneously. They are healthier, more comfortable, and operate at roughly **one-third the cost** of conventional systems.

We install our system on the ceiling because:

- The ceiling offers an **uninterrupted radiant surface** — no furniture or foot traffic in the way
- It **doesn't activate dust** that accumulates on floors
- It is far more **hygienic** than floor conditioning or forced-air systems

#### Features

- **Heating and cooling** in a single system
- **Multiple finishing options** — choose the surface that suits your interior
- **Standard size: 600 × 1200mm** — fits standard Australian suspended ceiling grids
- **Modular components** — easy to install, modify, and maintain

#### System Components

Our hydronic system uses modular, low-voltage components:

| Component | Specification |
|---|---|
| Circulation pumps | 40–85W variable speed, 24V DC (each pump serves 20–40m²) |
| Switching actuators | 24V DC (for hot & cold tank selection) |
| Mixing valve | 24V DC with flow meter and supply temperature sensor |
| Pressure tanks | 2 litre |
| Piping | PPR with brass fittings |

> **Note:** The control system is separate to the hardware listed above.

### Page: `/laboratory/environmental-service-pod` — Environmental Service Pod (ESP)

**Page Title:** Environmental Service Pod
**Subtitle:** All your building energy services in one modular system

#### What Is the ESP?

The Environmental Service Pod (ESP) is the first system of its kind to consolidate all required building energy services into a single, modular unit. It can be installed in both new and existing homes.

The ESP provides:

- **Heating and cooling** for the whole home
- **Domestic hot water**
- **Battery storage** to power home appliances and the heat pump
- **Real-time monitoring** of a personally customised indoor environment

By consolidating these services, the ESP is significantly more efficient than running each system independently — it capitalises on the synergies between heating, cooling, and storage. Every component has been carefully designed and tested for optimal efficiency with minimal energy waste.

#### ESP Modules (Off-Grid Example)

This example off-grid ESP consists of six integrated modules:

1. **MODBUS Control** — central system management and monitoring
2. **LiFePO4 Battery** — 7.5 kWh lithium iron phosphate storage
3. **48V DC Polyvalent Heat Pump** — simultaneous heating and cooling
4. **5 kW Inverter/Charger** — converts and manages power between solar, battery, and grid
5. **220L Hot Water Storage** — thermal storage for heating and domestic hot water
6. **220L Cold Water Storage** — thermal storage for cooling

#### The Polyvalent Heat Pump

Our heat pump consumes electrical input at an average Coefficient of Performance (COP) of **4:1** — meaning for every 1 kW of electricity in, you get 4 kW of thermal output.

But here's the key advantage: **this is a polyvalent heat pump that generates heating and cooling simultaneously.**

Standard regulation testing only measures one output at a time. When you account for both outputs together, the effective COP is **7:1** — making it one of the most efficient systems available.

#### Design Principles

- **Modular** — individual components are prefabricated and ready for simplified hookup on site delivery
- **Scalable** — the system adapts to different building sizes and energy demands
- **Consolidated** — fewer separate systems means less waste, lower cost, and simpler maintenance

### Page: `/services/residential/comfort-conditioning` — Comfort Conditioning

**Page Title:** Comfort Conditioning
**Subtitle:** Healthier, more efficient heating and cooling for your home

#### How We Condition Your Home

We use hydronic radiant ceiling panels — a fundamentally different approach to heating and cooling compared to conventional ducted systems or split units.

Rather than blowing hot or cold air around the room, our system radiates energy directly from the ceiling. This is how the sun warms the earth, and it's how the human body naturally exchanges heat with its surroundings.

#### Why Radiant Is Better

Your body exchanges energy through three mechanisms: radiation, convection, and breathing. Of these, **radiant transfer has the greatest impact on comfort**.

Radiant systems deliver both radiant and convective conditioning simultaneously, making them the most effective form of environmental conditioning available. They are:

- **Healthier** — no dust circulation, no dry air, no noise
- **More hygienic** — ceiling-mounted panels don't collect or disturb floor dust
- **More efficient** — operating at roughly one-third the cost of conventional systems
- **More comfortable** — even temperature distribution without hot or cold spots

#### What We Install

Our suspended hydronic ceiling panels provide heating and cooling in a single system. Each panel is:

- **600 × 1200mm** — fits standard Australian suspended ceiling grids
- **Modular** — easy to install, modify, and maintain
- Available with **multiple finishing surfaces** to match your interior

The system runs on low-voltage (24V DC) components including variable speed pumps, actuators, and mixing valves. It's quiet, safe, and simple.

#### Paired With Renewable Energy

Our conditioning system is designed to work hand-in-hand with solar PV, battery storage, and our polyvalent heat pump. This means your heating and cooling can run primarily — or entirely — on renewable energy.

### Page: `/services/residential/heat-pump-systems` — Heat Pump Systems

**Page Title:** Heat Pump Systems
**Subtitle:** Efficient heating, cooling, and hot water from a single unit

#### The Polyvalent Heat Pump

Our heat pump is different from conventional units. It is **polyvalent** — meaning it produces heating and cooling simultaneously, rather than one or the other.

For every 1 kW of electricity consumed, the heat pump delivers an average of **4 kW of thermal output** (a COP of 4:1). But because it generates hot and cold water at the same time, the real-world effective performance is closer to **7:1** — making it exceptionally efficient.

#### What It Powers

The heat pump is the core of our Environmental Service Pod and provides:

- **Space heating** — via our hydronic ceiling panels
- **Space cooling** — via the same ceiling panels
- **Domestic hot water** — stored in a 220L insulated tank

All from one compact, 48V DC unit.

#### How It Fits Into Your Home

The heat pump connects to two thermal storage tanks (hot and cold) and is managed by an intelligent MODBUS control system. Combined with solar PV and battery storage, it forms a complete home energy system that can operate independently of the grid.

### Renewable Energy & Net-Zero Strategy

> *This content works well on the main services page, the about page, or as a dedicated section on the ESP page. It provides the "big picture" vision.*

#### A Renewable Energy Solution

Our approach combines three pillars: **electrification**, **thermal storage**, and **hydronic conditioning**.

We harness solar PV energy, store electricity in batteries, and generate conditioned water using our polyvalent heat pump — stored in thermal tanks. This provides heating, cooling, and domestic hot water entirely from renewable energy.

The goal: **fully grid-independent buildings**.

#### A Staged Approach to Net-Zero

You don't have to do everything at once. Our system is designed to be implemented in stages, each one reducing your energy costs and carbon footprint:

| Stage | What You Get | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1** | No solar, no storage (baseline) | Full grid dependence |
| **Stage 2** | 10 kW solar PV | Significant daytime energy offset |
| **Stage 3** | + Polyvalent heat pump + two thermal storage tanks | Heating, cooling & hot water from solar |
| **Stage 4** | + 5 kWh battery | Evening energy coverage begins |
| **Stage 5** | + 10 kWh battery | Near grid independence |

#### The Environmental Impact

At scale, the numbers are significant:

- **12+ tonnes CO₂** reduced annually per house
- For 1,000 homes, that's **12,000 tonnes CO₂** — equivalent to permanently removing **4,700 cars** from the road
